Overview

A CUDA-accelerated Smoothed Particle Hydrodynamics (SPH) single-fluid simulation that can render ~10 million particles at around 30 FPS on current-gen hardware. The project focuses on high-performance particle simulation on the GPU and is mainly a personal learning exercise for me to get more familiar with CUDA programming in a enjoyable context.

I am currently working on further expanding it to support multiple mixable/soluble and non-soluble materials and solids. And in the future, I plan on improving the volume renderer (also done in CUDA) that is used to visualize the particles as is currently a bit noisy and slow, even without supporting multiple ray bounces/reflections yet.
